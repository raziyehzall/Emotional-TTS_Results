Emotional Speech Synthesis Results

Audio samples generated by the proposed emotional text-to-speech system under different datasets and emotion control settings.

Demo Description

This demo presents emotional text-to-speech (TTS) synthesis results obtained under different emotion control settings and evaluation datasets. Each sample includes the input text, an optional reference speech sample, and the corresponding synthesized speech generated by the model.

Emotion Control Modes

  • Reference-Based Emotion Control: An emotional reference speech sample is provided to the model. The model extracts emotional characteristics from the reference audio and generates speech that preserves the target speaker identity while mimicking the emotional style of the reference.
  • Emotion Vector Control: Only a neutral speech sample of the target speaker is provided. The emotional style is controlled using a predefined emotion embedding (emotion vector), allowing the model to synthesize speech with the desired emotion while maintaining speaker characteristics.

Evaluation Datasets

  • CV3Eval and EmoVoice: Evaluation is performed in a zero-shot setting. The speakers appearing in these evaluation samples were not seen during training. During synthesis, only a short speaker reference sample (approximately 5 seconds) is provided to adapt the model to the target speaker.
  • ESD (Emotional Speech Dataset): Evaluation is performed in a seen-speaker setting. The speakers were included in the training data. During inference, a short speaker reference sample (approximately 5 seconds) is provided to preserve speaker identity.

Displayed Content

  • Text: The displayed text corresponds to the linguistic content spoken in the synthesized audio sample.
  • Reference Audio: The audio used either for speaker adaptation and/or emotion conditioning, depending on the experiment setting.
  • Generated Audio: The speech synthesized by the proposed model for the displayed text.

The side-by-side presentation of reference and generated audio enables qualitative assessment of speaker similarity, emotional expression, naturalness, and intelligibility across different synthesis conditions.
